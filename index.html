ðŸ“‹ Import to Roblox

  1. VMF Import.

    • Step 1: Download Roblox Studio
    • Step 2: Add VMF Importer [Beta] Plugin

      VMF Importer [Beta] â€“ View on Roblox Plugin Store
    • Step 3: Bulk Import Images
    • Step 4: Save VMF with only 1 area active in VisGroups
    • Step 5: Use Import VMF [BETA]
    • Step 6: Once Imported you will need to replace Unions with Parts for accurate collisions.
      select all triggers by searching in studio explorer for "tag:hazardstrip001a" or whatever material you replaced triggers with then group together.
  2. OBJ Import.

    • Step 1: Import OBJ. Group meshes and name after errored meshes that were imported.
    • Step 2: Attach SurfaceAppearance to models with materials. use .mtl files to find texture to use.
    • Step 3: Simple Script to place Primary Part at world center for imported model.
      Replace "workspace["props_not_ready"]["Missing: cyberwave/arch_01"]" with your imported model.

      local model=workspace["props_not_ready"]["Missing: cyberwave/arch_01"] --[[ Model Goes Here --]]
      local PrimPart=Instance.new("Part")
      PrimPart.Parent=model
      PrimPart.Transparency=1
      PrimPart.CanCollide=false
      PrimPart.Anchored=true
      PrimPart.Name="PrimaryPart"
      PrimPart.Position=Vector3.new(0,0,0)
      PrimPart.Size=Vector3.new(3.2,3.2,3.2)
      model.PrimaryPart=PrimPart

    • Step 4: If the model requires Collision I duplicate the Primary Part. Delete duplicate meshes, Group it with name Collisions then move and resize to the shape of the model.
